# 15 Asian Cuisine Menu Items — High Protein, Low Fat

**Curated by:** Chansey (Nutrition Specialist)
**Goal:** Muscle gain, fat loss
**Date:** 2026-04-08

## 1. Salmon Sashimi (10 pcs)
**Cuisine:** Japanese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~290 kcal | 43g | 12g | 0g |

Raw salmon sliced thin — no rice, no sauce. One of the cleanest lean proteins available. Rich in omega-3s for muscle recovery. Order with soy sauce (low sodium preferred) and skip the mayo-based dips.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.6:1** — Top pick.

## 2. Tuna Poke Bowl (Brown Rice, No Creamy Sauce)
**Cuisine:** Hawaiian-Japanese fusion

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~450 kcal | 34g | 9g | 52g |

Build with: raw tuna (leanest poke protein), brown rice, edamame, cucumber, seaweed, ponzu or soy dressing. Skip mayo, spicy aioli, and crispy tempura topping. Tuna is among the lowest-fat fish proteins at ~1g fat per 30g protein.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.8:1**

## 3. Steamed Chicken Dumplings / Siu Mai (8 pcs)
**Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese)

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~280 kcal | 22g | 7g | 32g |

Steamed — not pan-fried or deep-fried. Pork-and-shrimp siu mai or chicken versions keep fat lower than pork belly varieties. The thin wheat wrapper adds minimal fat. Pair with chilli vinegar dipping sauce (not sesame oil-heavy sauces).

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.1:1**

---

## 4. Gai Yang — Thai Grilled Chicken
**Cuisine:** Thai

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 kcal | 38g | 11g | 5g |

Char-grilled marinated chicken (lemongrass, garlic, fish sauce). No frying. Breast or thigh cut — breast is leaner. Serve with a side of steamed jasmine rice and som tum (green papaya salad) for fibre without added fat. Avoid coconut-based curries and fried rice.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.5:1**

---

## 5. Miso Soup with Tofu and Wakame
**Cuisine:** Japanese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~80 kcal | 9g | 3g | 6g |

A light, high-protein accompaniment. Silken tofu provides plant protein; wakame seaweed adds iodine and minerals with near-zero fat. Best used as a starter or side to boost overall protein with minimal calories. Sodium is notable — good post-workout but moderate if watching salt.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.0:1**

## 6. Steamed Fish Fillet with Ginger & Soy
**Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese)

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~280 kcal | 38g | 6g | 4g |

White fish (barramundi, snapper, or cod) steamed whole or filleted with fresh ginger, spring onion, and light soy. No oil, no batter. This is the gold standard for low-fat, high-protein Asian cooking. The steaming method preserves protein integrity and keeps fat at a minimum.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 6.3:1** — Best on this list.

## 7. Chicken Teriyaki Bowl (Skinless, Steamed Rice)
**Cuisine:** Japanese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 kcal | 36g | 10g | 56g |

Grilled skinless chicken breast glazed in teriyaki sauce (soy, mirin, sake) over steamed rice. Request skinless — chicken skin adds 5–10g unnecessary fat. Teriyaki sauce adds some sugar but keeps overall fat low. Avoid the tempura add-ons.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 3.6:1**

## 8. Vietnamese Pho with Chicken (Pho Ga)
**Cuisine:** Vietnamese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~380 kcal | 32g | 6g | 48g |

Clear broth soup with rice noodles and poached chicken. The broth is bone-based but skimmed of fat. Chicken breast version keeps fat very low at ~6g. Load up on bean sprouts, fresh herbs, and lime — all near-zero calories. Avoid beef brisket (bo tai) pho which is higher in fat.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 5.3:1**

## 9. Korean Bibimbap (No Egg Yolk, Gochujang on Side)
**Cuisine:** Korean

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~520 kcal | 28g | 10g | 78g |

Rice bowl with seasoned vegetables, lean beef (bulgogi) or tofu, and a raw egg. Request egg white only to reduce fat. Gochujang (chilli paste) adds flavour with minimal fat — use it as dressing but request on the side to control quantity. The vegetables (spinach, bean sprouts, carrots, mushrooms) add fibre and micronutrients for minimal calories.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 2.8:1**

## 10. Edamame (Steamed, Lightly Salted)
**Cuisine:** Japanese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~190 kcal | 17g | 8g | 14g |

Complete plant protein — contains all essential amino acids. High in fibre, folate, and iron. Best as a starter or snack to boost protein intake. The fat present is mostly polyunsaturated (healthy). One of the few plant foods that qualifies as a high-protein option.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 2.1:1**

## 11. Tandoori Chicken (Skinless, No Butter Sauce)
**Cuisine:** Indian

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~290 kcal | 35g | 8g | 4g |

Clay-oven roasted chicken marinated in yoghurt, cumin, coriander, and chilli. No frying, no ghee. The yoghurt marinade tenderises the meat while adding negligible fat. Skinless breast version is extraordinarily lean at ~29g protein per 100g with only ~4g fat per 100g. Avoid butter chicken, korma, and any cream-based curries.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 4.4:1**

## 12. Pad Thai with Chicken (No Extra Oil, Extra Bean Sprouts)
**Cuisine:** Thai

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~480 kcal | 28g | 12g | 62g |

Rice noodles stir-fried with chicken, egg, bean sprouts, and tamarind sauce. Request minimal oil — traditional street-style pad thai uses less oil than restaurant versions. The egg adds complete protein. Bean sprouts bulk the dish with almost no calories. Avoid the peanut topping (adds ~8g fat per tablespoon) or request it on the side.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 2.3:1**

Silken tofu steamed or cold-served with soy sauce, a few drops of sesame oil, and spring onion. Tofu is a complete plant protein. The sesame oil adds flavour with only a small amount needed — request light drizzle. High in calcium and iron. Best as a side to complement another protein source.

## 14. Hainanese Chicken Rice (Poached Chicken, Steamed Rice)
**Cuisine:** Singaporean / Chinese

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~520 kcal | 40g | 14g | 52g |

Poached whole chicken served over fragrant rice (cooked in chicken broth). The poaching method keeps fat much lower than roasting or frying. Remove the skin before eating to reduce fat by ~6–10g. The ginger-scallion and dark soy dipping sauces add minimal fat. One of the highest protein-per-serve dishes in Asian cuisine.

**Protein:Fat ratio: 2.9:1**

## 15. Korean Sundubu Jjigae — Soft Tofu Stew (Seafood Version)
**Cuisine:** Korean

| Calories | Protein | Fat | Carbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~320 kcal | 28g | 9g | 22g |

Spicy soft tofu stew with seafood (clams, shrimp, squid) and silken tofu in a gochugaru-based broth. Seafood keeps fat very low while delivering high protein. The broth is chilli and anchovy-based — deeply flavourful with minimal oil. Add a raw egg into the boiling stew at serving for an extra ~6g protein. Avoid pork belly versions (significantly higher fat).

## Chansey's Asian Cuisine Tips

- **Steamed > fried** — always. Steaming is the default in Cantonese, Japanese, and Vietnamese kitchens. Frying adds 10–25g fat per dish.
- **Clear broths > coconut broths** — pho and miso are lean; laksa and Thai green curry are not.
- **Raw fish is your friend** — sashimi and poke bowls deliver the highest protein-to-fat ratio of any food court option.
- **Tofu and edamame** — best plant-based protein sources in Asian cuisine. Both contain all essential amino acids.
- **Avoid:** Tempura, karaage, tonkatsu, spring rolls, fried rice, and anything with coconut milk or heavy sesame oil dressings.
